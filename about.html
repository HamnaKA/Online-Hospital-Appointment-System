Prevention is better than cure remains true in terms of heart diseases to a great extent. Heart diseases are often considered to be disease of the latter part of ones life as the symptoms are mainly expressed in the fifth or sixth decades of life in majority of life. This loses the window of opportunity for prevention. In fact, fatty streak formation which is the beginning of fat deposition inside the arteries begins in the second decade of life and later on, the gradual accumulation of cholesterol, fibrous tissue and calcium occurs during third, fourth and fifth decades of life forming the atherosclerotic plaque.
